The 2026 Mercury take the floor for the first time tonight in Sioux Falls, South Dakota — the first WNBA game ever played in the state. It's a homecoming for head coach Nate Tibbetts (SD native, entering Year 3) and assistant Megan Vogel, both with deep ties to the area.

This is the first live look at a retooled, post-Sabally Mercury. Phoenix is coming off a 2025 Finals run (swept by Vegas), then watched Satou Sabally walk to the New York Liberty in free agency. GM Nick U'Ren responded with continuity: re-signing Copper, Thomas, Bonner, and Whitcomb. The big new addition is French forward Valériane Ayayi — a EuroLeague teammate of Alyssa Thomas at USK Praha.

Opponent: a dramatically remade Chicago Sky — without Angel Reese, who was traded to Atlanta on April 6 for two future first-rounders. Chicago's new core: Skylar Diggins, DiJonai Carrington, Kamilla Cardoso, Rickea Jackson. Year 2 under HC Tyler Marsh.

What to watch: Tibbetts has signaled a faster, more movement-based offense. Several Mercury players are still overseas and won't suit up. Expect a lot of camp-invite minutes. Don't read into the score — read the pace, the spacing, and how Ayayi looks running with AT.

Five things Phoenix needs to show tonight — preseason result is irrelevant, but these tells will matter.

01
Pace & Movement Offense
Tibbetts said this week: "We're going to play to the second and third side more. We're not going to hold it as much." Last year's offense leaned heavily on AT initiating; this year needs more flow without Sabally as a secondary creator.
02
Ayayi Integration with Thomas
Valériane Ayayi has played alongside AT at USK Praha in EuroLeague. Tibbetts compares her to a young DeWanna Bonner — positional versatility, multi-skill forward. First on-court look at that pairing.
03
Natasha Mack's Conditioning
Tibbetts highlighted Mack's offseason: "10 to 15 pounds of muscle… in a lot better shape." Conditioning was the limiter on her minutes. If the new build holds up, Phoenix has a real defensive anchor at the 5.
04
Defending Cardoso & Diggins PnR
Chicago's offense will run through Skylar Diggins–Kamilla Cardoso pick-and-roll. With Mack and AT, Mercury have rim protection — but the on-ball coverage scheme is what to watch. New defensive coordinator setup this year.
05
The Camp Battle
Phoenix brought 24 players to camp — two full rosters. Watch for Kara Dunn, Maggie Doogan, Yarden Garzon, Shay Ciezki — all undrafted but flagged league-wide as players who could surprise.

Tibbetts on April 21: "I don't think anyone will be back for Saturday's game." Multiple Mercury players are still finishing overseas commitments. Expect a depleted but expanded camp roster on the floor.

A Homecoming for Tibbetts
South Dakota native Nate Tibbetts brings the Mercury home for the first WNBA game ever played in the state. Assistant Megan Vogel also has SD ties. The Sanford Pentagon is in Sioux Falls and has hosted top-level NCAA events but never a WNBA game — until tonight.
Copper Faces Her First Franchise
Kahleah Copper played seven seasons in Chicago and won the 2021 WNBA title there. She's now a 4× All-Star centerpiece for Phoenix. Even in preseason, this is a meaningful first encounter against the franchise that built her — now without Reese, but with Diggins and Carrington as the new face.
Sky Without Reese — A Reset
Chicago shocked the league on April 6 by trading Angel Reese to Atlanta for two future first-rounders. The Sky pivoted hard — adding Diggins, Carrington, Jacy Sheldon, and Azurá Stevens. Year 2 under HC Tyler Marsh with a near-total roster reset around Cardoso.
The Sabally Hangover
Sabally averaged 19.0 PPG / 7.0 RPG in the 2025 playoffs before a Game 3 concussion ended her Finals. She signed with NY on Apr 11. Phoenix did not sign a like-for-like replacement — they're betting depth, continuity, and Tibbetts' system can absorb the loss.
A New CBA, A Compressed Camp
The new WNBA collective bargaining agreement compressed free agency and the draft into a sprint. Phoenix held back early before announcing a wave of re-signings on April 12. Camp opened April 19 — one of the shortest runways in WNBA history heading into May 9 opener at Vegas.
