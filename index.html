In her place: Valériane Ayayi, a French international forward who played alongside Alyssa Thomas at USK Praha in EuroLeague. Five things Phoenix must execute to pull the upset in Las Vegas. The Aces are 7.5-point favorites — beating that number, let alone the game, requires discipline in each of these areas.

01
Contain A'ja Wilson
Wilson is the 4× MVP, reigning champion, and just signed the largest contract in WNBA history. Last regular-season matchup (Aug 15, 2025): 30 PTS / 16 REB in an 86-83 Aces win. If she approaches those numbers, Vegas wins by double digits. AT must front her early, force rotations, and make Wilson work for every touch.
02
Win the 3-Point Battle
Mercury don't have the size or depth to outmuscle Vegas in the paint. Their path runs through spacing — Whitcomb, Copper, and Ayayi all need to be threats from deep. Copper shot 4-of-6 from three in the preseason opener. Sustaining that volume against a more disciplined Aces closeout unit is the offensive equation.
03
Take Care of the Ball vs Gray
Chelsea Gray turns turnovers into instant offense. She's one of the best pick-and-roll orchestrators in WNBA history. Akoa Makani and the Mercury guard unit must limit live-ball mistakes — each Mercury turnover compounds Vegas's already formidable fast-break advantage.
04
Get to the Free-Throw Line
In a game where the Aces have home-crowd energy and a ring ceremony glow, Mercury need to draw fouls and stay on the line. AT is an 80%+ FT shooter. Copper, Mack, and Linskens inside — every contested touch in the paint matters. Foul management on Wilson is equally critical: don't gift her extra possessions.
05
Weather the Aces' Mid-Game Run
Vegas is notorious for explosive scoring bursts that ice games. The Finals Game 3 Q3 (Aces 21, PHX 16) showed how quickly the margin widens. Mercury must hang within striking distance through three quarters — don't let one bad 4-minute stretch compound into a 15-point hole they can't close from.
